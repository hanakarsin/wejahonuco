Uasin Gishu ODM Leaders End Internal Differences, Prepare to Welcome Raila in Solidarity

Publish date: 2024-07-31
  • The Orange Democratic Movement (ODM) party leader Raila Odinga is slated to tour Uasin Gishu county as he advances his Azimio la Umoja clarion call
  • The region is presumed to be Deputy President William Ruto's stronghold
  • Raila is yet to formally declare his presidential candidature despite the indications

ODM officials in Uasin Gishu are preparing for the outfit's leader Raila Odinga who is slated to undertake a charm offensive mission starting next week.

The grassroots party functionaries are said to have met and resolved the in-house issues in readiness for Raila's tour.

The region is a known stronghold for Deputy President William Ruto who seeking to succeed President Uhuru Kenyatta in the next year's vote.

"We have had consultations, we have net grassroots party officials and we have all agreed on how we will be running the affairs of our party. Most importantly, we are welcoming Raila Odinga with his Azimio la Umoja clarion call," said Kipkorir Menjo.

Menjo was seconded by Songok, another party official, who said the region was ready and prepared for Raila's clarion call.

"We are ready for Azimio la Umoja and Raila Odinga who will be ouf fifth president," said Songok.

The planned visit comes barely a week since the former prime minister was in North Eastern where he was drumming support ahead of the 2022 general election which he is yet to formally declare his interest.

As he earlier stated, Raila will be traversing the whole country before officially declaring his presidential candidature which he has already signalled.

Ruto in Raila's backyard

At the same time, his presumed chief rival William Ruto has been visiting different parts of the country to mobilise support.

Unlike Raila, Ruto has already made it clear that he will be vying for the country's highest office.

A fortnight ago, the deputy president was in Nyanza to counter Raila's perceived supremacy.

On the Saturday of September 25, Ruto launched a massive campaign in Migori which was warmly received by the locals.

Ruto pitched the tent in Awendo town, Migori county.

Consultative meeting

He held a consultative meeting with Migori county grassroots leaders at the Sony Sugar Complex in Awendo constituency in the company of Governor Okoth Obado.

''We must commit to building national political parties that carry the national agenda and can deliver a government with a national face. It should be a party of choice in Migori as is in Turkana, and any other part of Kenya. That is the outfit that will tackle tribalism and bring meaningful political stability to our country,'' Ruto explained.

He was accompanied by MPs Mathias Robi, Marwa Kitayama, Silvanus Osoro, Ndindi Nyoro, former Awendo MP Jared Kopiyo, Omingo Magara, among others.

'We have agreed to broaden and deepen the United Democratic Alliance (UDA) party’s networks in the Nyanza region. I have come here because I know that we have hustlers here in Awendo. True or not true?" Ruto posed.

Church fundraiser

He said that he had toured the region to lead a fundraiser at Awendo Central SDA Church.

"Even as we organise ourselves here on earth, we should also think about how best we can go to heaven. Therefore, I am ready to listen to your concerns and listen to you too so that we can agree on how we shall move forward together," Ruto said while addressing a mammoth crowd in Awendo town.

Obado said that the second in command was welcome to visit any part of the county perceived as Raila's stronghold.

"We are going to the church then we shall have a very big peaceful rally to declare that power belongs to the people. I thank you all for welcoming our visitors today," Obado said.
