Celebrity Cop Sammy Ondimu Urges Fellow Police Officers to Work Professionally:&quot;Ukitenda Mabaya Uko

Publish date: 2024-06-22
  • Sammy Ondimu has advised his fellow police officers to uphold professionalism in their services
  • The police officer spoke up on the recent increasing cases of police brutality
  • He told his colleagues that those who will be found guilty of going against the law will carry their own crosses

Celebrated police officer Sammy Ondimu is one of the men in service who don't fear speaking out their minds.

Taking to Facebook on Tuesday, August 17, the revered officer who doubles up as a singer composed a letter to his colleagues urging them to be professional in their work.

His remarks were inspired by the recent increasing cases of police brutality in the country.

Ondimu shared screenshots of Inspector General of police Hillary Mutyambai's post where he had stated that the police officers who were involved in the gruesome murder of the Kianjkoma brothers were suspended to pave way for prosecution as was directed by the DPP Nordin Haji.

He warned his colleagues that those who will be found guilty of using excessive power will face the wrath of the law as individuals.

Ondimu, the 'Polisi ni Binadamu' advocate, added that he always prays for God to give him the wisdom of serving others.

"To all my colleagues, kindly work professionally. If you break the law, you are alone and you will face it alone at the court. I pray every day for God to give me the wisdom of serving Kenyans," reads part of the letter.

Sammy Ondimu urges Kenyans not to fear marrying police officers

In another article previously published on TUKO.co.ke, Ondimu urged Kenyans not to fear getting married to members of the National Police Service (NPS).

He dismissed the common notion that the men and women in the service are bad and corrupt people whose only intention is to kill.

Ondimu said police officers are also responsible parents and he explained that people should not be defined by their occupation but by their personalities and behaviour.
