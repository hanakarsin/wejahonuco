Caroline Kangogo: DCI Says Tracking Killer Cop Is Hard Because She No Longer Uses Her Phone

For more than a week now, corporal Caroline Kangogo is still on the loose after managing to escape several police dragnets.

As her pursuit continues, the Directorate of Criminal Investigations (DCI) now says tracking the killer police officer is hard as she no longer uses her known phone.

Consequently, the DCI and other security agencies have resorted to physically searching for the fugitive, accused of killing two men.

"It has been challenging tracking down the officer because she no longer uses her phone. However, our team has stepped up the hunt," said Nakuru County Criminal Investigations Officer Anthony Sunguti.

Kangogo is the main suspect in the murder of Constable John Ogweno in Nakuru and a civilian, Peter Ndwiga, in Thika, who met their deaths in less than 24 hours.

Special team mobilised

To boost and intensify the search, the rogue police officer described as armed and dangerous, DCI mobilised a multi-agency team from elite units in the force.

The team comprises the police, military and those from the investigations agency.

Being unreachable on the phone, the unit will physically conduct surveillance on major roads, including Nairobi-Nakuru-Eldoret and Nairobi to Mombasa.

The team will also conduct a search in hotels, lodges and other places thought the fugitive could be hiding.

Travelling six counties

Previous reports indicated that the corporal's phone signal had shown that she had visited six counties and investigators believe she was using public service vehicles while also concealing her face.

On Wednesday, July 7, her phone signal was traced to Kahawa Wendani at 2.15am, and on the same day, she visited Kericho, where she is believed to have called someone with a borrowed phone.

Kangogo later travelled to Kisii, then Iten, Elgeyo Marakwet county, which is also her home town.

On Thursday, July 8, her phone signal then showed she was at Kenlands area Nakuru county at 3am and exactly 12 hours later, her last phone signal traced her to Eldoret.

After the Juja murder, the corporal withdrew KSh 20,000 from her account and another KSh 40,000 from her police Sacco account.

She is evading the police dragnet by switching off her phone while she is in transit to blind the law enforcers on her movements.
