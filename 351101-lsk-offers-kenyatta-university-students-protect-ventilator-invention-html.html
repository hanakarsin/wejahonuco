LSK offers to help Kenyatta University students protect their ventilator invention

Publish date: 2024-02-21

- The Law Society of Kenya is in search of Kenyatta University students who invented ventilators

- Society president Nelson Havi said if their invention cannot be protected early enough their idea may end up benefiting someone else

- The students said they are able to assemble at least 50 ventilators in a week

The Law Society of Kenya has offered free legal services to Kenyatta University Students who invented ventilators to help coronavirus patients.

The society is now looking to get in touch with the students to have their invention protected before someone steals their idea.

In a Twitter post by LSK president, Nelson Havi, the society appealed to Kenyans to help them get in touch with the inventors.

"Could the 16 Kenyatta University students who have assembled 500,000 prototype medical ventilators quickly get in touch with us. We need to protect your invention before some of those 90 years old youthful government officers deprive you of your right," said Havi.

As earlier reported by TUKO.co.ke, a group of 16 engineering students from Kenyatta University (KU) developed the much needed respirators in the fight against the COVID-19 pandemic.

The students from different faculties embarked on the venture to develop the ventilator's prototypes within a week putting in 18 hours a day.

The apparatus were unveiled on Saturday, April 11, through the institution's innovation and incubation centre saying they could manufacture 50 ventilators in a week.

Another team of engineers also made a locally assembled ventilator at the cost of KSh 84,000.

Paul Kariuki and Samuel Kairu made a ventilator to help coronavirus patients breathe with ease.

The x-factor in their prototype is that it can be used in every part of the country since it is powered by a car battery.

The country needs the equipments the most with only 259 functional units available and over 140 of them are already in use. This means only 119 are available for use.
