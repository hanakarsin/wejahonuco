Kirinyaga county assembly has denied reports doing rounds on social media purporting that it has passed the Constitutional Amendment Bill 2020 dubbed the Building Bridges Initiative (BBI).

The assembly's Speaker Anthony Waweru said the House was on recess and will resume plenary sitting on February 26.

In a statement dated Wednesday, January 27, Waweru, however, admitted to having received the Bill but insisted the ward representatives were yet to deliberate on it.

“The County Assembly of Kirinyaga has not considered the Constitutional amendment Bill 2020 (BBI),” read the statement in part.

The speaker further stated that the House in consultation with the people of Kirinyaga and within the confines of the law will consider the Bill and submit its decisions to the speakers of both the Senate and the National Assembly as required by section 257(6) of the Constitution.

1.14 million signatures

The constitutional amendment report entered another stretch after the Independent Electoral and Boundaries Commission (IEBC) approved 1.14 million signatures.

The commission's chairperson Wafula Chebukati said a total of 1,715,386 supporters' signatures had been captured and digitised to allow for easy verification as on Tuesday, January 19.

In a press statement on Tuesday, January 26, Chebukati further confirmed there were 13,272 signatures with either invalid or missing ID names while 4,777 supporters forgot to include their signatures.

Road to referendum

The Commission has since submitted the draft Bill to the 47 county assemblies for consideration within 30 days from the day of submission.

Each Speaker will then forward their respective county's decisions to both the National Assembly and the Senate for further consideration in line with Article 257 of the Constitution.

The BBI draft Bill must be supported by at least 24 county assemblies for it to move to the next stage.
