Caroline Kangogo: Fugitive Cop Met John Ogweno, Peter Ndwiga in Club Before Killing Them

  • Ogweno, who was attached to Kasarani Line Police Station in Nakuru county, was found dead inside his car a day later
  • Detectives are pursuing the three men and probing to establish if they have a hand in the killings

Details have emerged that runaway cop Caroline Kangogo met both John Ogweno and Peter Njiru Ndwiga at a club near Nakuru's Dog Section Police Station on Sunday, July 4.

Kangogo met the duo whom she is suspected of killing alongside three other unidentified people, Daily Nation reported.

Ogweno, who was attached to Kasarani Line Police Station in Nakuru county, was found dead inside his car a day later.

Discussions

According to some officers who saw them, Kangogo and the men sat together and held discussions before Ogweno left alone.

After leaving the venue, the constable was reported to have taken a taxi to Nakuru CBD before he proceeded to Merica Hotel to meet another person.

He later took a boda boda to his house at Kasarani Police Houses where he shared with Kangogo on block 9.

The three other men later arrived at the house accompanied by Kangogo.

It was, however, not clear what happened between the time they joined Ogweno and the time the 28-year-old's body was discovered in his car on the morning of Monday, July 5.

Detectives are pursuing the three men and their link, if any, to the murder of the officer.

Njiru's death

Kangogo who is still on the run lured Peter Njiru to a hotel in Kimbo, Juja area, at around 4pm on July 5, about 200 kilometres away from the scene of Ogweno's murder.

She shot him at night before escaping from the room.

Njiru's body was discovered the following morning in the hotel room.

Kangogo being protected by colleagues

The investigations and pursuance of the fugitive cop took a new twist after detectives revealed she was part of a crime syndicate operating in the Rift Valley.

Fresh details indicate that the cop, accused of killing two men, was involved in shady deals with her colleagues, who are now protecting and aiding her escape police dragnets.

The detectives said the killings might have been a case of a deal gone south, adding that other police officers apart from Kangogo were implicated in the incident.
