Missing Ugandan Woman Found Dumped in Septic Tank at Her Marital Home

Publish date: 2024-04-16
  • Immaculate Mary Blessing Asio, 62, went missing on January 6, 2021
  • A body believed to be hers was recently recovered in her marital home's septic tank
  • Detectives also discovered the slain woman's diary in which she painted the picture of a tumultuous marriage to her husband Francis Onebe
  • In the diary, the woman expressed fears over her life and wondered why an unnamed person/people hated her

A Ugandan woman who has been missing since January 2021 has been found dead and her body dumped in her marital home's septic tank.

The remains believed to be of Immaculate Mary Blessing Asio,62, who went missing on January 6, 2021, were retrieved from a septic tank located in the home she lived with her husband in Munyonyo, Kampala.

According to a report by Daily Monitor, police officers discovered Immaculate's body which was in a sack a few days ago, after receiving a tip-off from a former security guard who worked in the home.

Another body believed to be of her househelp was also discovered in the tank.

According to investigators, the couple's home had been renovated making it hard to find clues to what could have possibly happened to the woman.

Her husband Francis Onebe who has been under police custody since the discovery of his wife's body had, a few months ago, accused security officers of abducting his wife.

“If they have killed her, then let them give me the body, I bury because I am being tormented. If she is alive, let me at least talk to her and that is all because I am being tormented so much," he said in March 2021, two months after his wife's disappearance.

Discovery of woman's diary

According to the law enforcers, a diary believed to be of Immaculate and which also painted the picture of a tumultuous marriage was discovered and is providing crucial leads in the probe of her death.

The 62-year-old woman documented the physical and emotional assault from an unnamed person and also expressed fears over her life.

For instance, she once wrote that she felt unsafe in her own house further wondering why they did not like her.

"Every day I feel I am not safe yet in my own house. I know they don’t like me after my struggling as a housewife for all these years? Why do you hate me like this, my dear," one of the pages of her diary read.

She had, once also fled her marital home and moved to a rented house but she later moved back in with her husband after he promised to change.

Tumultuous marriage

The couple met in 1982, but in 1993, they went their separate ways. Around this time, Onebe married another woman and sired four children.

However, in early 2000, he reconciled with Immaculate, who was now richer owing to her businesses and they even wedded and formalised their union which brought forth one daughter.

Besides domestic violence, detectives believe familial enmity could have led to the businesswoman's death.
