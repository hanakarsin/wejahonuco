China blocks WHO team from entering country to probe COVID-19 origin

Publish date: 2024-04-29

- The first case of COVID-19 was reported in Wuhan city in December 2019

- In June 2020, more than 100 countries signed an accord asking World Health Organisation (WHO) to probe the origin of the deadly disease

- On January 5, a team of 10 international scientists left for Wuhan to carry out the investagations but were blocked

- China’s minister for Foreign Affairs, Hua Chunying, said there were some procedures that were supposed to be complied with before the process commences

Authorities in China have blocked a team of scientists from the World Health Organisation from entering Wuhan city to probe the origin of the novel coronavirus.

The deadly virus was first reported in Wuhan in December 2019 and spread across the globe claiming millions of lives and bringing the world's economy to its knees.

This prompted WHO to assemble a team of scientists to investigate the origin of the dreaded virus after more than 100 countries gave the move the green light in mid 2020.

The go ahead

On December 15, WHO announced a team of 10 international scientists were set to travel to Wuhan in January to carry out the investigation.

This was confirmed by the health body on Tuesday, January 5, when the team left. However, things turned ugly hours later after it emerged Beijing had blocked the team from entering Wuhan.

Tedros Adhanom Ghebreyesus, the WHO Director-General, said two scientists who were part of the United Nations team had already left their home countries for Wuhan when they were told that Chinese officials had not approved the necessary permissions for them to enter the country.

Disappointment

Tedros expressed disappointment in the manner in which President Xi Jinping's government had suddenly changed its initial position and locked out his team.

He noted his office had been in constant communication with top Chinese officials regarding the need to probe the origin of the virus and they had all agreed on the procedures and modalities to be used.

"I have been in contact with senior Chinese officials and I have once again made clear that the mission is a priority for WHO and the international team. I am very disappointed with this news," he said.

"Fulfill necessary procedures"

China’s Ministry of Foreign Affairs, Hua Chunying, however, dismissed the reports and insisted China was committed to the course and would facilitate the WHO team as agreed.

Hua noted President Jinping's administration has been cooperative and transparent since the outbreak of coronavirus and would continue being open to the end.

He, however, noted there were some procedures that were supposed to be complied with before such engagements can run smoothly.

"In order to ensure that the international expert group that comes to China can work smoothly, it is needed to fulfill the necessary procedures and make relevant specific arrangements. The two sides are still negotiating about this,” Hua said as quoted by CNN.
