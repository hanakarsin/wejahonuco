BBI: Report roots for tax free business for SME start ups

Publish date: 2024-07-08

- The Building Bridges Initiative (BBI) proposed a tax-free holiday for young entrepreneurs as an incentive for them

- Kenyans decried to the BBI taskforce the high taxation which they termed the main hindrance to start-ups

- Entrepreneurs will now enjoy tax-free seven years from the opening of their enterprises

Budding and young entrepreneurs will now operate their businesses tax-free for the first seven years of setting them up if recommendations in the Building Bridges Initiative (BBI) sail through.

Youth starting micro and medium-sized enterprises will enjoy a tax-free holiday as part of the incentive to support them.

According to the Standard, Kenyans decried the high taxation experienced by young entrepreneurs that often hindered the success of their businesses to the BBI task force, compelling the team to make the suggestion in the report.

"Minimising taxation of new and small businesses by giving them a tax holiday of at least seven years as a support to youth entrepreneurship and job creation," the report reads.

This comes at the height of numerous challenges on startup owners who have continuously lamented on lack of credit and also harsh taxation.

Kenya Revenue Authority is working to tax online business which many youths resort to due to lack of formal employment.

Meanwhile, the Central Bank of Kenya (CBK) has lowered its benchmark lending rate from 9% to 8.5%, a move it says will help spur economic growth.

Lowering the rate is expected to see banks slash their lending rates which will, as a result, encourage uptake of loans.

The BBI report was handed over to President Uhuru Kenyatta on Tuesday, November 26.
