If Ida Allows, Find Another House in Mt Kenya, Priscila Nyokabi Jokingly Tells Raila

Publish date: 2024-02-26
  • On Tuesday, former prime minister Raila Odinga held a meeting with the Mt Kenya Foundation bringing together business leaders from Mt Kenya
  • The leaders in the meeting praised Raila as they pledged their support for his candidature in next year's presidential race
  • Raila has been meeting leaders from the Mt Kenya region where he has recently camped seeking support for his 2022 presidential candidature

The meeting between the Mt Kenya Foundation (MKF) and ODM chief Raila Odinga could not end without a light moment when the former premier was invited to the mountain to find another "house".

The jest was driven by former Nyeri MP Priscilla Nyokabi who suggested Raila had been welcomed in the region contrary to the assertions that he was deemed an adversary by the region's electorate.

"If Ida allows, unawezachukua nyumba ndogo hapa Mt Kenya ndio when someone says umezeeka, unamwambia uliza bibi mdogo (If Ida allows you can find an alternative house in Mt Kenya so ghat you disapprove those arguing you have become old)," said Nyokabi.

Mandela moment

Nyokabi who also serves in the National Gender and Equality Commission (NGEC) added that the time had come for Raila to assume the leadership of the country stating that he is the best fit after President Uhuru Kenyatta.

"You are our Joe Biden, our Mandela and we look forward to a bright future, and we women are ready to work with you" she said.

She also urged Raila to prioritise the passage and implementation of the two-thirds rule hopefully in his tenure beginning next year.

Another request made to the presidential hopeful is revenue allocation which Nyokabi said should match the Mt Kenya population and also the protection of water towers in the region.

Raila receives support

Raila was on a tour of the vote-rich Mt Kenya region as he continues to make inroads in the region perceived to be Deputy President William Ruto's stronghold.

Four governors from the region, Nderitu Muriithi (Laikipia), Francis Kimemia (Nyandarua), Lee kinyanjui (Nakuru), and James Nyoro (Kiambu) vow to back Raila for the 2022 presidency.

The leaders said they would roll out a massive campaign to sell the ODM leader as their preferred presidential candidate of choice.

"We shall plan and coordinate a structured engagement with the region focusing on elected leaders, community leaders, business leaders, farmers’ organisations, the faith organisations and key opinion leaders and shapers in the region," Muriithi said in a statement on behalf of the county bosses.

Raila's warm welcome

On Sunday, September 26, the ODM leader attracted huge crowds in Laikipia who turned up to welcome him when he toured the region.

Raila was thrilled by the warm reception he was accorded, and the excitement was painted on his face, and the locals who jammed to reggae music that blasted from an SUV a sound system was mounted on.
