Raila Says He Has Been Using Herbal Concoction, Steaming Daily to Manage COVID-19

Publish date: 2024-04-08

- Raila Odinga tested positive for the coronavirus in mid-March and was admitted to The Nairobi Hospital

- The ODM boss said he was put on drugs for the first five days of his hospitalisation after which he received some herbal mixture

- He further revealed he has been steaming daily in the evening noting that COVID-19 treatment and management require self-discipline

The Orange Democratic Movement (ODM) leader Raila Odinga has narrated his three-week battle with COVID-19.

Raila, who tested positive for the virus on Wednesday, March 10, said he was put on drugs for the first five days of his hospitalisation, after which he used a herbal drug when he got discharged.

Speaking at the State House on Thursday, March 1, the former premier, although he recovered from the infection, he revealed he had been steaming daily to manage the infection.

"I received a mixture that was more of a herbal drug. Secondly, I have been steaming a lot in the evening. Then I was on oxygen for a while. It is a process, and one needs to be disciplined and follow the doctor's instructions," he stated.

Earlier in the day, Raila accompanied President Uhuru Kenyatta to inspect development projects in Nairobi and Kajiado counties.

In Nairobi, the duo made an impromptu visit to the Green Park Bus Terminus on Haile Selassie Avenue, which currently undertaken by the Nairobi Metropolitan Service (NMS).

"I am now out. God is good. Today I had the chance to be out for the first time. I want to thank my brother (Uhuru) for visiting me at home. Kenyans can now see me. I have been in critical condition for the past three weeks. I am now out and I thank God," said Raila.

It was the first time Raila physically addressed the media after testing positive, although the seasoned politician had, on March 31, held a meeting with the ODM top leadership.

"The meeting reaffirmed ODM's commitment to the spirit of the handshake...We also affirmed ODM's unwavering commitment to the Building Bridges Initiative and the Constitution of Kenya Amendment Bill 2020," he said in a statement.
