  • A 90-kilogramme bag of maize is retaining at KSh 2,800 at the moment

Kenyans will have to dig deeper into their pockets to put ugali on table as prices of maize flour rise as a result of shortage of maize supplies.

As of May 2021, a two-kilogramme packet of the staple food in most households was averagely retaining at KSh 90, but by July, the price had risen to above KSh 100.

The increase in prices was attributed to a decline in the supply of quality maize in the market from the neighbouring country Tanzania.

This is as a result of the produce failing to pass the aflatoxin test at the Kenya-Tanzania border, Business Daily reported.

Finance Act, 2021

The higher prices of flour will pile more pressure on the cost of living after the cost of other household items and basic services skyrocketed after the government moved to implement the Finance Act, 2021.

The Act, effected after President Uhuru Kenyatta signed Finance Bill 2021 into law, introduced amendments to various tax-related acts of parliament and related statutes in the public finance sector.

As a result of the new tax laws, the cost of various products and services shot up.

For instance, Safaricom calls now cost KSh 4..87 per minute in peak hours (8am to 10pm) and KSh 2.50 per minute in off-peak hours (10pm to 8am) from KSh 3.50 and KSh 1.80 respectively before the implementation of the new tax.
