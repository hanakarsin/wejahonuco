IMF Compels Kenya to Disclose Secret Shareholders in Companies Receiving Gov't Tenders

International pressure is mounting on Kenya to disclose faces behind several companies that do businesses with the government.

The International Monetary Fund (IMF) demanded the full disclosure of the shareholders of business entities benefiting from the award of government tenders.

The IMF deputy managing director Antoinette Sayeh noted the move would help fight wanton corruption in the government and also preventing the loss of public funds to individuals, Business Daily reported.

The government through the office of the Attorney-General had in January 2021 gave companies until July to publish details of shareholding, directors as well as their physical address.
