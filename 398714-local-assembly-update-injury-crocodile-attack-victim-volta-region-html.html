Lucky to be alive: Woman attacked by crocodile in viral video doing well after treatment

Publish date: 2024-03-15

- An update has been given by the Akatsi North District Assembly on the viral video of a crocodile attack

- In the video, the female tourist was spotted getting pounced upon by a crocodile she attempted to take a picture with

- According to the local assembly, the woman only sustained minor injuries and is currently doing well

The Akatsi North District Assembly has given an update on the lady who was captured in a viral video getting physically attacked by a crocodile.

The woman whose name was not disclosed had gone on a tour at the crocodile pond and attempted to take a photo with the crocodile which pounced on her and attacked her.

Many viewers were concerned as they could not deduce whether the woman survived the attack, was brutally injured or otherwise.

Fortunately, according to Graphic.com.gh, it has been clarified that the female tourist who was attacked by the crocodile at Ave Dakpa in the Akatsi North District of the Volta Region sustained a minor injury and was doing well.

The tourist was quickly rushed to the hospital and was discharged the same day.

The local government has also begun investigating the circumstances that led to the attack while also pledging to give full details once the probe is over because of the public attention that the incident has attracted.

"Our preliminary investigations show the incident happened before the festive period but the victim did not sustain any serious injuries," District Chief Executive for the area, Prince Amuzu told a local publication in Ghana.

As reported by TUKO.co.ke, the viral video showed the crocodile emerging from a pond as the tourists watched on with great delight.

However, their joy quickly turned to a complete state of distress and a cry for help as the crocodile launched an attack on them while they posed for photos.

Although the crocodile initially remained calm as the tourists took turns to take photos with it, it suddenly attacked the woman who touched it from the side.
