EACC summons Baringo county MCAs over chaos during BBI Bill debate

Publish date: 2024-02-17

- EACC summoned the Baringo county Speaker, deputy speaker, 13 MCAs and three Seargent-at-arms

- The commission said it had launched investigations into the conduct of the wards reps during the BBI Bill debate

- The MCAs are expected to visit EACC's offices between February 22 and 24

The Ethics and Anti-Corruption Commission has summoned a section of Baringo county Members of county assembly over the chaos that erupted during the debate of the Building Bridges Initiative (BBI) Amendment Bill 2020.

Baringo became the first county to shoot down the BBI after a stormy session that was characterised by yelling and disagreements that nearly turned physical.

In a statement on Thursday, February 18, the anti-graft agency summoned the county Speaker David Kiplangat, his deputy Jacob Cheboiwo, 13 MCAs and three sergeants-at-arm to appear on different dates between February 22 and 24.

"The commission is investigating the conduct of Members of county assembly during proceedings of February 11, 2021, at the Baringo county assembly chambers," the statement read in part.

As TUKO.co.ke reported, 30 Baringo MCAs voted overwhelmingly to reject the Constitutional Amendment bill being fronted by through the BBI in a heated session that saw them nearly go physical.

Only five MCAs voted in support of the bill; Baringo is the only assembly that has so far rejected the bill.

Baringo's verdict is, however, in contention and a section of MCAs have moved to court to challenge it.

The KANU war reps were unsatisfied with the outcome filed a petition at Kabarnet Law courts arguing the process was rushed and did not give the public a chance to participate as required by the constitution.

On the flip side, 11 counties passed the bill with Nairobi, Laikipia and Vihiga being the latest counties to adopt the draft.

At least 24 county assemblies out of the 47 are supposed to adopt the legislation before it moves to the bicameral House for further debate and voting.
