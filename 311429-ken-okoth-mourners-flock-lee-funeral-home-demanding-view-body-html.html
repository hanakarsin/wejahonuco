Ken Okoth: Mourners flock Lee Funeral Home demanding to view body

Publish date: 2024-06-22

In a bid to show their love for their deceased Member of Parliament, tens of Family, friends and Kibra constituents have flocked Lee Funeral Home demanding to see MP Ken Okoth's remains.

Their demands came barely an hour after Okoth’s brother Imram Okoth assistant said the MP's body will not be viewed by people outside his family on Friday, July 26 or Saturday July 27.

There was heavy police presence around the area while the facility’s security managed to confine the crowd outside the morgue

."We are currently working on plans of the funeral. What we want the people to know is that there will be no public viewing of the body at Lee Funeral home except for the family members," he said

The 41-year-old legislature succumbed to Colorectal cancer in the afternoon of Friday, July 26.

According to his brother Okoth was unable to utter even a word when he was taken to Nairobi Hospital on Thursday, July 25, morning.

Okoth had sought treatment in Paris, France, stayed there for close to five months and returned to Kenya about a fortnight ago.

He was taken ill and rushed to the Nairobi Hospital but his health deteriorated and he was taken to the Intensive Care Unit and placed on life support.

He suffered massive organ failure and his family asked doctors to switch off the life support machine, a request that the MP had communicated to them before his death.
