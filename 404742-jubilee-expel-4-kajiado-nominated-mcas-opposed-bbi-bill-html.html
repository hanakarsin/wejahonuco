Jubilee to expel 4 Kajiado nominated MCAs who opposed BBI bill

Publish date: 2024-05-25

- The quadruple Onesmus Ngogoyo, Faith Sianka, Winnie Lanoi and Lucy Mathenge had been nominated by the party two weeks ago

- Even though the Kajiado County Assembly endorsed the BBI bill, the MCAs were singled out for voting against party position

- In February, Jubilee had expelled six nominated senators allied to DP William Ruto over disloyalty but the Political Parties Disputes Tribunal suspended the decision

The Jubilee Party is set to expel four nominated Members of County Assembly (MCAs) in Kajiado county after they openly opposed the Building Bridges Initiative (BBI) bill against the outfit's position.

The quadruple Onesmus Ngogoyo, Faith Sianka, Winnie Lanoi and Lucy Mathenge had been nominated by the party two weeks ago and would face disciplinary action over their alleged disloyalty.

A letter by Jubilee Secretary General Raphael Tuju to the ward representatives accused them of gross misconduct and violation of the party's code of conduct.

Even though the Kajiado County Assembly endorsed the BBI bill, the MCAs were singled out for voting against party position yet they were expected to remain loyal to it after it awarded them jobs.

Tuju asked the MCAs to defend themselves within seven days failure to which the party would take action against them without further reference.

Daily Nation, however, reported their fate was sealed after they attacked President Uhuru Kenyatta and BBI during a meeting attended by Deputy President William Ruto.

6 nominated senators expelled

In February, Jubilee had expelled six nominated senators allied to the DP over disloyalty.

The six senators who were expelled include Millicent Omanga, Isaac Mwaura, Seneta Mary Yiane and Gona Christine Zawadi.

Others were Waqo Naomi Jilo, Prengei Victor and Iman Falhada Dekow.

"The NMC is exercising its mandate as provided for under Article 7(2A) of the party constitution and relying on other enabling provisions of the Party Constitutions has expelled the senators. "The expulsions are with immediate effect and have been communicated to the Senate and Registrar of Political Parties," a by Tuju statement read in part.

The lawmakers got a reprieve after the Political Parties Disputes Tribunal suspended the outfit's decision.

The tribunal’s chairperson, Desma Nungo, in her ruling on Tuesday, February 9, said the lawmakers were subjected to an unfair trial by the party's disciplinary committee.
