A Huduma Namba message has rekindled the hope of one family in Mwingi, Kitui county, of uniting with their kin who has been missing for more than 45 years now.

The family of Benjamin Malii Livu received a message that his card was available for collection, reviving their hopes that he might still be alive.

After receiving the card from Imba location chief John Mbiti, the family noted that it bore his details and a picture that seemingly matched his current age, prompting them to intensify their search.

"The Huduma Namba card has renewed our hope of reuniting with my elder brother Benjamin. It is the clearest signal that he could be alive," said Kamene Katunduma, his younger sister as quoted by Nation.

Katunduma argued that if he could register for Huduma Namba, he must have been alive in 2019 when the government rolled out the first phase of the exercise.

Livu left his home in 1974 in his youthful days to look for green pastures in Nairobi.

His parents did not bother as it was normal for young men to leave their rural homes to look for employment in urban centres.

However, his long absence raised eyebrows when he failed to return home in 2014 during his mother's burial as expected.

Anthony Muhati

In a separate story, Anthony Muhati received a call from someone from his homeland in Kakamega who told him that his kin believed he was dead.

The phone call prompted Muhati to go back home 14 years after leaving behind his family and moving to Trans Nzoia county, where he built a life of his own.

According to a report by The Standard, the 58-year-old only wanted to clarify to his family that he was still alive and that the person they buried in his place was a stranger.

After disappearing in 2007, many of his family members could not quite remember his physical appearance. When they heard a body that looked like that of Muhati was lying in a Kitale morgue sometime in 2020, they took the first bus to the mortuary to find out if it was really him.
