Governor Obado's late lover and other top 20 Google searches by Kenyans in 2018

Publish date: 2024-02-01

The 2018 FIFA World Cup and the slain Rongo University student Sharon Otieno and ex-lover to Migori Governor Okoth Obado respectively topped Google searches by Kenyans in 2018.

Sharon was abducted and later found murdered in a thicket near K'Odera Forest in Homa Boy county on Tuesday, September 5, and police linked at least five suspects including the governor to the gruesome murder.

During the one-month period in which Obado was remanded, Kenyans followed minute by minute development both on mainstream media and social media.

Soccer enthusiasts on the other hand searched for information regarding match fixtures, scores and group standings.

Self-styled National Resistance Movement general Miguna Miguna came in position three whereas Kenya Certificate of Primary Education results made it to the fourth position in the search engine.

Miguna was deported, flown back then redeported to Toronto, Canada on grounds he renounced his Kenyan citizenship although he denied the allegation.

Others who trended in Kenya were Citizen TV's Jackie Maribe who together with her boyfriend Joseph Irungu were both arrested in connection to killing of 28-year-old Nairobi business woman Monica Nyawira Kimani.

Milimani Law Courts released Maribe on bail but Irungu remains locked up.

Google also published top trending local personalities which again saw Sharon on top, followed by Miguna, Maribe, Embakasi MP Babu Owino, State House Spokesperson Kanze Dena, Obado and late multiparty icon Kenneth Matiba.

Perhaps Kanze's appointment as the Secretary, Communication and State House Spokesperson at the Executive Office of the President is the sole reason why Kenyans were interested in her.

It should be remembered on Friday, March 2, the Court of appeal upheld Owino's election as Embakasi East MP.
