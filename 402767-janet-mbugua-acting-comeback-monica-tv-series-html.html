Janet Mbugua makes acting comeback on Monica TV series

Publish date: 2024-06-11

- Former TV news anchor Janet Mbugua will be making an appearance on Showmax's Monica

- The mother of two started acting in 2014 on Rush, a Kenyan drama show, but she left the acting scene as soon as the show came to an end

-Mbugua explained she took a pause on her acting career as she had a demanding job and a young family to prioritise

- The star will make her debut Friday, January 12, on the fifth episode of the show where she will play the role of a powerful female deputy governor.

Media personality Janet Mbugua will be gracing the screens again but this time around, with a different role as an actress.

The celebrated TV siren got into the acting scene in 2014 with a lead role in the AMVCA nominated Kenyan drama, Rush.

And she disappeared from the acting field as quickly as she got in after the show came to end in the first season.

Now, the beautiful Mbugua is making a comeback, and this time with a better performance. She said:

‘’My character is like Olivia Pope without the marital scandals.’’

For six years, the mother of two turned down numerous acting projects that she came across due to having a young family and a demanding career.

‘’I have been offered roles over the years but I had to say no for various reasons. I did not have a family of my own when I starred in Rush, so at the time, I did not mind the crazy filming hours.But when I started growing my family, I was very cognizant about not stretching myself. Suddenly I was a new mum, an anchor, a brand ambassador, a moderator, a wife. That is a lot.

And while some people can do it and I admire them, some of us just feel like we need to take a backseat and focus on certain things,’’ she explained.

However, the TV queen’s comeback was expected when she was approached to make an appearance on TV show Monica season three.

The show features Brenda Wairimu who depicts a young woman who schemes devious ways to make it to the top.

Mbugua believes that this role came at the perfect time despite being not sure if it would suit her.

‘’I always feel like you cannot necessarily double back. My background is in news anchoring. Acting was something I did because I like to embrace new challenges and experiences. To be honest, I thought I had gotten that bug out of my system although I enjoyed it of course, and then this role came my way,'' she added.

She also thanked her new co-stars for being kind, supportive and understanding that this is not her everyday job.

Her new role will be the most intriguing character on the show this season.

She will be playing Jesse, a fierce and powerful woman who is not afraid of laying her cards on the table and one who does not show any need of taking control despite having her own personal skeletons.

Jesse comes in as the new deputy governor, a position that was being eyed by Monica, who until now has been on top of her scheming game.

And if Jesse’s first day in the office is anything to go, then it seems that Brenda’s Monica role has finally met her match in Mbugua's Jesse character.

For an actor who is only on her second role, it feels so right the ext-TV anchor gets to play a monumental role such as Jesse, a female deputy governor in a country that is yet to actualize the two-thirds gender rule.

According to Mbugua, there are still lots of opportunities for women to pursue especially now when many females roles are still being sexualised.

“Let us diversify these roles and have more female actors in leadership roles that are again not tied to sexual scandals only, but those that provide authority and role-modeling,’’ she added.

She will be making her debut on Friday, February 12, on the fifth episode of the Showmax show.

The former News anchor sometime back explained how politicians and colleagues asked her for sexual favours while she was a TV girl.

The mother of two said during her stint in the media, politicians propositioned her knowing too well she was married and that often, bagging a job without bending over backward and greasing a few palms is quite an extreme sport for most women in the media industry.
