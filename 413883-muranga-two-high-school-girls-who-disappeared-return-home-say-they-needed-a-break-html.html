Murang'a: Two High School Girls Who Disappeared Return Home, Say They Needed a Break

Publish date: 2024-06-10

- Mercy Njeri and Clarin had disappeared from their homes on Sunday, May 23, causing panic among family members

- The two, who are childhood friends, had disagreed with their parents, stated they needed a break from their homes

- One of the girls, Clarin Njeri, said they were putting up at their friend's place in Thika

Two female high school students who disappeared from their homes following a disagreement with guardians have returned home safely.

Mercy Njeri, who hails from Kirinyanga'g county, and Clarin Njeri (Murang'a) ran away from their homes on Sunday, May 23, send their families into a panic.

The duo is enrolled at Murang'a Secondary School.

According to NTV, the childhood friends have been putting up with their friend in Thika, citing they wanted some time out from their homes.

One of the girls, Clarin, said they were pushed to run away from their homes following a disagreement with their parents.

"For that one week, we were staying at our friend's house in Thika, and during that period, we had peace that we needed so much," Clarin said.

She also said they were pushed to come out of their hiding place after the news about their disappearance was all over.

"Sometimes we are pushed to do things which later we come to realise that they were not beneficial to us, I just want to urge my fellow youths that if they have problems at home, they should find a way of solving since running away from home is not a solution," she added.

We were afraid because the news about our disappearance was all over and tormented for the fear our parents were going through.

Ann Njeri, Clarin's grandmother, could not hide her joy after seeing her granddaughter back home alive.

"I thank God my granddaughter is back alive; I was worried about her life, even neighbours are at peace now that she has returned," the old woman said.

In a separate story, TUKO.co.ke reported that Butere Girls' High School in Kakamega county was panic over the whereabouts of a Form One student who has been missing since Saturday, May 15.

According to the school's administration, the student was in the company of other Christian Union students, never returned after attending church prayers.

Maureen Nzilani Mutua's distressed mother told journalists her daughter, who was supposed to be at school, called her and said she was in Mumias city, then disconnected the call.
