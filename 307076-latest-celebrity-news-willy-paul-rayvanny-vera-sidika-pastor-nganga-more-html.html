5. R Kelly charged with 11 more sexual offences

Popular music artist R Kelly has been charged with 11 additional sexual offences on top of other ten accounts.

According to Chicago Sun-Times, the new charges date back from 2010 and relate to sexual abuse and assault of a child aged between 13 to 16 years which is enough evidence is available will have more consequences than the others. In the state of Illinois, they are the most seriously taken felony class, and he may end up being imprisoned for six to thirty years.

In the old charges, the award-winning rapper pleaded innocent and was released on bail. If enough evidence supports these ten accounts which involve four affected people, three of whom are minors, he could be jailed for around three to seven years.

Speaking on his official Twitter account, his lawyer Steve Greenberg confirmed the new allegations saying that they do not relate to the old case.

He was recharged in an existing case, same alleged victim and time (a decade ago) It changes nothing. These are the same conduct, just charged differently, the same alleged victim, same time frame, same facts. We expect the same results, he wrote.

The rapper has been accused by dozens of women for abusing them. Early this year, a documentary series titled, “Surviving R.Kelly” showed some victims which he allegedly abused talking of their experiences. However, R. Kelly remained firm and denied all the reports.

6. Actress Njambi is expecting a baby, talks about her depression struggle

Popular House Helps of Kawangware actress, Njeri Gachomba popularly known as Njambi is expecting a second born child.

Despite the actress keeping most of her private life news low from the media and public, she opened up on how she suffered a miscarriage.

I might not be ready to talk about it, but I still remember, she stated.

She later urged Kenyans to remember all the children that were born sleeping and those who never made it for long.

A baby born after a miscarriage or loss of a baby is referred to as a Rainbow baby. Today we remember all the babies born sleeping, or we’ve carried but never met, those we’ve held but couldn’t take home, the ones that came home but didn’t stay. Make this your status if someone you know has suffered the loss of a baby. The majority won’t do it, because miscarriage, stillbirth, SIDS, and SUDC is a taboo subject. Break the silence, she urged Kenyans.

Njambi narrated the pain she felt when she lost everything.

I remember when I took this photo; this was the day I lost everything I’ve ever worked for. I had sat on the stairs as they took everything from my house. My mum was so stressed land my brother, as usual, was cheering me up. I remember he was telling me about how I was used to having my bathroom in my bedroom asasa na rrudi lykutumia choo na kkila mtu (my brother though), she revealed.

The actress explained how her daughter reacted when she got into the new small house.

I felt like my life was ending I mean I just got up now am being put down again. I remember otam came back from school and she was shocked, ‘haukuniambia tunahama! She was asking where our things are especially her toys…typical, she wrote.

She then narrowed down to how she battled with depression after losing all that was hers. Njambi didn’t like how youths were ending their lives due to love, jobs, and money.

Anyway, it wasn’t supposed to be a long post but anyways since am half way…can i just say something about depression and how sad it is that young people are ending their lives because of love, money and jobs,” the actress said adding, “I’ve tried it all taking pills, countless times, I’ve tried to commit suicide, I’ve wanted to jump off my balcony a million times. The Kawangware actress concluded by advising depressed people who feel like taking their lives away because of life challenges.

If you ever feel depressed and you feel like you want to end your life please find a purpose, everyone has a purpose for living. I think the reason am still alive am still surviving is because of someone somewhere who is going through a lot and has no one to talk to, my DM is always open. You know, depressed people are advised to talk about their issues, but it's never that easy for me because I hate talking about my problems.
