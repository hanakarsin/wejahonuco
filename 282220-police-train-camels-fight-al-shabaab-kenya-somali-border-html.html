Police train camels to help fight al-Shabaab on Kenya-Somali border

Publish date: 2024-04-26

- At least 50 camels are being trained to undertake patrols

- The move is aimed at reducing deaths of police officers targeted with IEDs

- They will be used to patrol parts of North eastern and areas where cattle rustling is rife

Police in terrorist hotspots in northern Kenya have come up with an uncanny way of dealing with militia.

A new police unit was announced on Wednesday, August 8, and it is understood the police will be using camels to patrol border lines.

About 50 camels are being trained to be effective enough to be used by the pioneer camel patrol unit under the Border Patrol Unit of the Administration Police Service.

The improvisation became necessary after it emerged that terror attacks had become prevalent in far-flung areas with security personnel being the biggest casualties.

Use of improvised explosive devices which are mostly planted on the ground also made it necessary for officers to devise new ways of patrolling terrorist hotspots.

Since 2016, up to 70 officers have lost their lives as a result of the explosives.

The trained camels will also go a long way in aiding the patrolling of the North Eastern region which is prone to cattle rustling.
