Jubilee MPs Attack Raila's Lack Of Record As Prime Minister

Publish date: 2024-03-14

Members of Parliament allied to the Jubilee coalition on Friday, August 14, 2015 took a swipe at opposition leader Raila Odinga over his 'lack of record' in western Kenya as the debate on the controversial Uganda sugar deal rages.

The Members of Parliament who accompanied William Ruto to western Kenya accused Raila of having done nothing to save Kenyas struggling sugar industry.

While referring to Raila's strong opposition of the Uganda sugar deal signed by Uhuru, the Jubilee coalition MPs said Raila has no record to show during his tenure as prime minister.

READ ALSO: ‘We Will Send Sugar Farmers To Camp In Statehouse’ Kakamega MCAs Claim

The Garissa MP said that the problems facing Kenya's sugar industry started when Raila Odinga was Prime Minister and therefore he should not pretend to lecture the government.

Raila has been leading the opposition in criticising Uhuru Kenyatta's apparent signing of a deal that will allow Uganda to export sugar into Kenya.

The opposition claims that the deal is a blow to the struggling Kenya sugar industry and bring suffering to Kenya sugar cane farmers.

But the Jubilee Coalition MPs said the deal will benefit Kenya and  accused Raila of speaking for sugar cartels while hiding behind farmers's interest.
