Nigerian female gospel singers and their stories

Publish date: 2024-03-06

Gospel music is an inevitable part of Nigerian culture. This industry has survived quite challenging times, but today it is a fast-growing and promising part of the Nigerian music industry.

Most famous female Gospel singers in Nigeria

Gospel artists in Nigeria are as appreciated as the top Nollywood actors. Below is a list of the most famous and talented female gospel singers in Nigeria who inspire their listeners with captivating voices and deep lyrics.

1. Onos

Onos is a gifted Nigerian Gospel singer and songwriter. She was born into a family of eight. She comes from the Niger Delta Region. The future singer earned a diploma as French translator. Presently, she is married to Mr. Kunmi Ariyo.

This year Onos’s name was included in all the possible charts in Nigeria. She entered the music ministry at the young age of eight. The singer can be described as a seeker of God, worshiper with an angel voice.

Being regarded as one of the best Nigerian Gospel artists, Onos had the honor to share the stage with such Gospel top singers as Fred Hammond, Israel Houghton, Chevelle Franklyn, Don Moen, Ron Kenoly, Phil Driscoll and Micah Stampley at The Experience 2010 music platform and with Jessica Reedy at the Corporate Elite Event at the Eko Hotel and Suites 2012.

Onos is an inspiring worshiper. Her songs create an incredible atmosphere of God’s presence. Her debut album ‘Keep Moving’ was released on February 28th, 2010. Recently, Onos released a sophomore album NO LIMITS and a few singles which have topped the gospel charts. Also, she released an animated video clip for her hit single “Alagbara”. 2. Sinach

Born Osinachi Joseph, Sinach is a Nigerian Gospel award-winning singer, songwriter, and senior worship leader at Believers' Loveworld International. Sinach has become a famous gospel artist whose songs are popular in Nigeria and beyond its borders, namely, in Kenya, Uganda, Barbados, South Africa, the UK, the USA, and other countries.

This talented vocalist is one of the most blessed Nigerian female Gospel singers of our time. As a songwriter, Sinach believes that writing from her heart can bring healing, hope, encouragement and strengthen the faith of the listeners. That’s why her songs are internationally known, they cut across nations and borders. Sinach wrote over 300 inspiring songs. She has recorded four audio and two DVDs: Chapter One, I’m Blessed, From Glory to Glory and Shout It Loud. Recently, she released a new album and a new video clip “Nothing is Impossible”. Presently, Sinach is signed to LoveWorld Records. She is also a key member of the music team at Christ Embassy. Here are several Gospel hits Sinach is widely-known for:

3. Ada

Since Gospel alternative-rock singer ADA hit the Nigerian music industry, her name was always on the top of the charts. Ada; music style is unique and light. Ada’s first step on the path to fame was her single BOBO ME.

Ada is simply incredible. Her Gospel music is an eclectic mix of Afro-pop, hip-hop and alternative rock. A brand-new Gospel music helped Ada Ehi to carve a niche for herself. Her music can be sweet, pure, fresh, original, lifting and powerful at the same time which opened to her a door into the world of the professional Gospel music industry in Nigeria and brought acknowledgment and recognition.

Born Ada Ehi, the singer studied chemical and polymer engineering at the Lagos State University where she met members of the Believers’ Loveworld Campus Ministry and Pastor Chris Oyakhilome. Since she met Pastor Chris Oyakhilome, the gospel of Christ seemed to jump out of the pages and became a living everyday reality. She has never looked back ever since. Ada signed on FreeNation Inc. and became a member of Pastor Chris Oyakhilome’s music team. Ada actively ministered with the Pastor performing her inspiring songs for millions of people around the world.

4. Pat Uwaje King

Pat Uwaje (also known as Mama Africa) is a famous Nigerian Gospel singer and songwriter. Pat Uwaje emerged from the gospel group Midnight Crew, an award-winning gospel music group best known for their hit song “Igwe”.

6. Yetunde Joyce

Yetunde Joyce is one of the most talented and creative Nigerian gospel artists. The Ireland based music star is an inspirational African Afro Folk singer, songwriter and music promoter. She is also a certified Project Manager and Event Consultant.

Yetunde Joyce way to becoming a successful songstress was not easy. As a singer with no music education, Yetunde Joyce has worked hard to become a respectable singer and built a great career. She cooperated with various international Gospel singers like Sonnie Badu, vocal tutors like Helen Walsh, Russell Smith, and music producers and directors like Wole Oni. Yetunde Joyce is a passionate singer whose songs reveal her strong personality and undeniable faith which she is using to reach the believers by her Afro Folk sounds.

7. Lara George

Lara George was born in Lagos into a family of five children. Lara became passionate about music when she was a member of the choir in school.

It is interesting that Lara George, one of the classiest Gospel artists in the world, didn’t plan to build a singing career. Lara George planned to pursue her career in architecture but, in the end, her passion for music won.

In 1997, she joined the “Rock solid” group which belonged to a campus fellowship called Rock Foundation Mission, and together with such talented singers as Ty, Emem and Dapo she formed KUSH (Kinetically Ushering Salvation into Hearts and Homes), one of the earliest modern Gospel music groups in Nigeria, which received numerous international awards. The group was very popular in the early 2000s, especially, for the single “Let’s Live Together”.

After KUSH broke up, Lara started her solo career. “Your dreams come true” was the first studio album to be released by Lara in 2007

Lara George turned out to be a multi-talented singer with a captivating voice, producer and music director. Her music professional career began in 1997, but she released her first hit single “Ijoba orun” in 2007. This single was a hit, and it still enjoys a huge popularity on radio stations.

In 2008, Lara George released a new album “Forever in my heart” that won the following awards:

  • 2008 Voice of the year award at the Nigerian Music award
  • 2009 Nigerian Gospel music awards nomination for “Female artiste of the year”

In July 2009, Lara George released a self-titled album Lara George (2009) that came with 14 tracks and featured Lord of Ajasa, Pat Uwaje. In 2012, Lara George released one more studio album titled “Higher”. recently, the Nigerian Gospel singer released her fourth album titled "A Slice Of Heaven”.

For Lara, it is important that her personal experiences influenced her songs. Lara George is best known for songs like:

Lara George got married to Gbenga George, a legal practitioner, and is blessed with a son Adeoba Alexander George.

8. T.Y. Bello

T.Y. Bello (born Toyin Sokefun-Bello) is a famous Nigerian Gospel singer, songwriter, and photographer.

T.Y. Bello is a member of the Nigerian photography collective, Depth of Field. T.Y. Bello is known to be an official photographer to President Goodluck Ebele Jonathan, the Former President of the Federal Republic of Nigeria. In past, T.Y. Bello worked for Thisday fashion magazine. She is also known as a philanthropist.

Before she started pursuing her solo career, she was one of the members of the KUSH, the Gospel band group mentioned above.

In the year 2008, T.Y. Bello released her debut studio album titled Greenland. This album took the star two years to develop, it was produced by Mosa Adegboye. This album won the Nigeria Music Award as well as the Sound City Award.

In 2011, T.Y. Bello released her second album “The Future”. The lead song from the album that has the same name inspires the Nigerian youth to be the change they are seeking. The Nigerian Ministry of Youth Development compiled an 8-minutes video, and T.Y. Bello featured in this video. In 2014, TY Bello released her third studio album titled “The Morning Songbook” that is available on SoundCloud. The album features collaboration with M Sugh and Fela Durotoye.

T.Y. Bello’s the most popular songs include:

  • Jesu Jesu
  • Thirsty
  • Yahweh

In July 2011, T.Y. Bello was honored by a non-profit organization Communication For Change organization in a five-part documentary film titled RedHot.

T.Y. Bello is married to Kashetu Bello, and they gave birth to twin boys named Christian and Christopher.
