Soita Shitanda's widow asks MPs to honour their burial pledges

Publish date: 2024-08-18

- Soita Shitanda's widow has sought to remind all 349 MPs that they owe her family KSh 1.7 million that they pledged to cover burial expenses

- Speaker Justin Muturi had called upon Majority and Minority leaders Aden Duale and Thomas Mwadeghu to follow up with their coalition members to pay up their pledges

- Ten days after Shitanda's burial, Duale now says he will not be following up on the task

Betty Shitanda, the widow of former politician Soita Shitanda, has called on Members of Parliament to honour pledges they made to her family to assist them in the funeral arrangements, amounting to KSh 1.7 million.

She made the request via her member of parliament, Malava MP Malulu Injendi.

The MP rose in parliament on Tuesday June 14, 2016 and said Mrs. Shitanda had contacted him a day earlier, asking when she would receive the cash in her account.

The Malava MP worried that it was a hard task to pursue the MPs to honour their pledges, especially after the funeral had taken place.

All the 349 legislators were to contribute KSh 5,000 per person, and speaker Justin Muturi had called upon Majority leader Aden Duale and Minority whip Thomas Mwadeghu to ensure their members paid up the monies.

But on Tuesday, Duale said he would not be following up on the task since the former housing minister was not a sitting MP at the time of his death.

The Minority leader on his part chose to call on those who were willing to contribute in terms of personal contributions, to do so.

The former Housing Minister and chairman of Agricultural Development Corporation (ADC) was laid to rest ten days ago in his Kakamega home, in a ceremony that was attended by a host of politicians led by President Uhuru Kenyatta and Deputy President William Ruto.
